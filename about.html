About

I'm Emanuele — MSc Finance graduate and currently in my third year of a Mathematics degree at Universidad Complutense de Madrid.

My background sits at the crossroads of finance and mathematics. I spent the first years of my career thinking about markets and risk; now I'm spending time going deeper into the mathematical foundations that make it all rigorous.

I'm drawn to problems where structure matters — whether that's the structure of a probability space, a financial market, or a dataset. I code mainly in Python and R, and I write in LaTeX more than I'd like to admit.

Outside of academics, I follow markets closely, read widely across mathematics and economics, and try to keep up with the literature on stochastic processes and their applications.

Based in Madrid, Spain. Open to conversations about quant research, model development, or anything at the intersection of math and finance.
