Global Equity High Tech Future Fund Invesco, 2024

Designed a UCITS-compliant ETF incorporating 33 positions across three high-conviction sectors: semiconductors (48.24%), aerospace & defence (25.14%), and quantum computing (20.33%). Stock selection was driven by a multi-factor quantitative screening process combining ESG scoring, R&D investment ratios, and a proprietary data driver — progressively filtering the universe from 195 candidates down to the final portfolio. As Risk Analyst, responsible for sector correlation analysis, risk indicator modelling, and backtesting the strategy against a synthetic benchmark composed of MSCI AC World indices — with the fund consistently outperforming across the backtested period. The strategy was presented to Invesco's management and selected for future client offerings.

Client Well-being Index N26 Bank, 2023

Designed a Financial Well-being Index for N26's client base, built on a hierarchical fuzzy inference system implemented in MATLAB. The model aggregates 14 financial variables through a multi-level architecture of fuzzy logic rules — defining membership functions, linguistic variables, and IF-THEN inference rules across intermediate nodes before converging into a single index score. Survey data was collected, normalised, and fed into the MATLAB Fuzzy Logic Toolbox across two cohorts (2022–2023), allowing comparative validation of the model's outputs year-over-year. The final report was presented at N26's headquarters and covered by major national media.

Active Share of Spanish Equity Investment Funds Universidad de Zaragoza, 2023

Final degree dissertation analysing the Active Share of 154 Spanish equity investment funds over a 20-year period (1999–2020), using the IBEX-35 as benchmark. The project involved building the full calculation methodology from scratch — first in Excel, then rewriting it in Python to handle a dataset of approximately one million rows. The findings showed a clear upward trend in Active Share over time, suggesting that Spanish fund managers have increasingly distanced themselves from the benchmark in pursuit of higher returns. Published in the University of Zaragoza's official repository.
